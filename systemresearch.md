# **Introduction**

## **Project Context**

The modern fashion landscape has made efficiency and precision critical for the survival of traditional tailoring businesses. Shop owners, designers, and staff often face administrative fatigue from managing manual measurements, tracking complex job orders, and coordinating with multiple textile suppliers. Tailoring services, such as bespoke garment construction, alterations, and custom uniform production, help professionalize the local fashion industry and meet the rising demand for personalized apparel, making them an essential part of today’s service economy.

Significant expansion has been observed in the tailoring management industry both globally and within the Philippines. According to Global Industry Analysts (2025), the international apparel management software market reached nearly $12.8 billion in 2024, while the Philippine garment sector saw a 6.5 percent annual expansion. This growth indicates that digital evolution has become a vital part of garment manufacturing and business operations. As a result, more tailoring enterprises are seeking integrated systems to meet increasing customer demand and ensure supply chain stability.

A majority of small to medium-sized tailoring shops in Davao City continue to rely on manual processes despite the clear growth of the industry. In several local establishments, customer measurements and sales are recorded in handwritten notebooks, while order tracking is handled through SMS or personal visits. Inventory and procurement records are often maintained separately using paper-based systems. While these traditional methods are common, they become inefficient as the volume of orders and complexity of designs increase.

The continued use of manual approaches frequently leads to a variety of recurring operational challenges. Disorganized production schedules and misplaced records often occur due to the lack of a centralized tracking system. Managing both walk-in customers and bulk orders, such as company uniforms, becomes difficult without real-time oversight of fabric and thread inventory. Staff also encounter challenges in tracking tailor assignments and monitoring fulfillment stages, especially during peak seasons. These issues often result in longer waiting times, data loss, and reduced customer satisfaction.

Fragmented data storage further hinders the ability of shop owners to monitor overall business performance. Since measurements, payments, and inventory logs are maintained separately, shop owners struggle to generate accurate reports, evaluate tailor productivity, and track deposits effectively. Customer engagement is also limited, as manual systems lack automated notifications and consistent history tracking essential for customer retention. According to Revillod (2024), implementing automated management systems significantly improves satisfaction by ensuring data accuracy and transparent tracking.

A growing body of research emphasizes the necessity of adopting digital solutions to sustain small enterprises. According to Lantz and Hjort (2024), automated systems are essential for maintaining service consistency and building long-term client loyalty in competitive markets. By moving away from manual guesswork, businesses can better manage long-term contracts and maintain the high level of organization required for professional growth.

To address these issues, the proponents are developing SUTURA: A Web-Based Subscription Tailoring Business Management System with Supplier Management. SUTURA serves as a digital partner that consolidates order tracking, stock alerts, and financial reporting into a single platform overseen by a centralized administrator to ensure stability. By moving away from manual guesswork, the system aims to simplify operations for shop owners, reduce mistakes, and help the local tailoring industry in Davao City truly grow and professionalize.

## **Purpose and Description**

Traditional garment businesses often struggle to maintain efficiency while relying on manual workflows for daily operations. Shop owners frequently record measurements in physical notebooks and track materials using paper logs, which leads to misplaced data, stock shortages, and fragmented communication. These manual inefficiencies not only hinder productivity but also make it difficult for owners to monitor business performance accurately, ultimately affecting the long-term sustainability of the craft.

To modernize these operations, this study proposes the development of SUTURA: A Web-Based Subscription Tailoring Business Management System with Supplier Management. The system provides a unified digital hub that simplifies and organizes the garment production process for owners, staff, and clients. By transitioning from manual notebooks to a centralized database, measurement histories and job statuses become instantly accessible, allowing the business to professionalize its management without losing focus on craftsmanship.

A tiered subscription model drives the platform, enabling tailoring shops to select a service plan that aligns with their specific operational needs. The system supports various levels of access, ranging from fundamental customer and job tracking in the Basic Plan to integrated inventory management and automated invoicing in the Pro Plan. For larger enterprises, the Premium Plan offers comprehensive tools such as multi-branch support and public digital portfolios to showcase garment designs.

Staff efficiency is enhanced through a management dashboard that organizes both walk-in and scheduled orders based on real-time availability. The system allows personnel to verify measurement versions, assign specific tailors to tasks, and monitor every production stage from cutting to final quality checks. All payments are processed and logged automatically, while labor costs and material usage are tracked to ensure accurate productivity records and financial transparency.

The project also features a dedicated web portal where customers can explore and compare various tailoring shops to find the one that best fits their style and needs. Users can browse through different shop profiles, view available garment designs, access their personal measurement history, and track the live progress of their orders through various fulfillment stages. Integrated notifications provide automated booking confirmations and pick-up reminders, eliminating the need for manual follow-ups and physical receipts.

Platform integrity is maintained through a structured administrative oversight process for all registered tailoring centers. While shop owners can immediately utilize internal management features upon registration, certain public-facing tools, such as the design gallery, are overseen to ensure service stability across the platform. This approach allows businesses to modernize their internal workflows immediately while maintaining a high standard of digital presentation for the customers.

## **Scope and Limitations**

**Scope of the Study**

This study covers the design and development of SUTURA: A Web-Based Subscription Tailoring Business Management System with Supplier Management, a cloud-based platform developed using React.js, Laravel, and MySQL. The system is intended to be used by four primary user groups: platform administrators, tailoring shop owners, staff, and clients within Davao City’s local garment sector. The platform adopts a centralized and scalable subscription-based architecture to ensure consistent data management while supporting the professionalization of traditional production workflows and the streamlining of supplier coordination for local tailoring enterprises.

The Subscription Module enables tailoring shop owners to register their businesses and select from a range of tiered service plans the Basic, Pro, and Premium based on their operational needs. Each subscription is managed through the system to provide role-based access for staff and designers, allowing them to curate business profiles and showcase design portfolios. This module replaces manual registration processes and provides a structured, scalable framework that allows local shops to manage their digital tools and user accounts efficiently.

The Customer Job and Fulfillment Order Module allows tailoring staff to manage and monitor the status of garment orders through defined stages such as intake, cutting, sewing, and final quality check. The system records all transaction details, including customer measurement versions, material requirements, and assigned tailor tasks, ensuring transparency and real-time tracking from the initial request to the final release. This module digitizes the traditional workflow, replacing manual notebooks with a structured system that prevents data loss and minimizes production backlogs.

The Appointment Module enables authorized personnel and clients to schedule, manage, and monitor all bookings related to tailoring services, such as fittings and consultations. It captures essential details including the appointment type, preferred date and time, and assigned staff, ensuring that all shop activities are systematically documented on a digital calendar. The module maintains a structured schedule to prevent overbooking and provides automated notifications to both staff and clients, supporting better time management and shop accountability. It also allows the generation of schedule summaries, providing owners with clear visibility into daily workloads and upcoming client meetings.

 The Inventory and Supplier Module enables tailoring shop owners to maintain adequate stock levels of fabrics, threads, and accessories by defining minimum inventory thresholds and tracking material usage in real time. Additionally, this module provides a dedicated interface for managing supplier information and procurement histories, highlighting low-stock items and supporting coordination with textile providers to prevent production delays.

The Admin Module provides a centralized repository of all registered tailoring businesses, managing the overall platform environment including subscription plan definitions, user account permissions, and service stability. This module enables platform administrators to oversee the activity of multiple tailoring centers by monitoring subscription statuses and system-wide data logs, while ensuring that each shop operates securely within its authorized service tier.

The system operates as an online, web-based platform requiring stable internet connectivity and is accessible through standard web browsers on both desktop and mobile devices. The system can only facilitate the design, implementation, and testing of its identified functional modules. While the platform enables digital record-keeping and communication, its performance remains dependent on server uptime and the accuracy of the data manually encoded by authorized users.

**Limitations of the Study**

While the platform provides an extensive suite of management tools, the development of SUTURA is shaped by specific constraints that outline its functional reach, technical requirements, and the practical scope of its deployment for local tailoring businesses.

The system is strictly confined to software-based management and does not include hardware integration, such as automated body-scanning devices, RFID tags, or digital measuring tools. All customer measurements and inventory stock levels must be manually encoded by authorized users.

As a web-based solution, a stable internet connection is required for all real-time updates and dashboard functionalities. Consequently, the system does not support offline data processing or background synchronization when network connectivity is unavailable.

Operational reliability depends entirely on the correctness of information maintained by authorized personnel. Disruptions caused by fabric delivery delays, tailor absences, or unrecorded manual adjustments may still affect the production flow despite automated scheduling validations.

The payment module tracks transaction details and deposits but does not include native integration with third-party payment gateways for direct fund transfers. External financial reconciliation and banking settlement verification are outside the scope of the system.

The reporting module is limited to operational and performance-based summaries. It does not include predictive analytics, AI-driven forecasting, or advanced business intelligence. Furthermore, notification delivery depends on third-party services, which may result in occasional delays.

The tracking of business utility costs, such as electricity bills and equipment maintenance, is not included as the system focus is primarily on production and inventory management rather than full-scale overhead accounting.

The management of employee payroll, including salary calculations and attendance-based wage distribution, is outside the functional scope of the platform.

While the Supplier Management module facilitates procurement, it does not provide a dedicated external portal for suppliers. All interactions, such as supply requests and inventory arrivals, must be managed internally by tailoring staff without direct integration into vendor databases.

Finally, the platform does not include government compliance processing, such as tax filing automation or business permit validation. It is restricted to managing the internal workflows of tailoring businesses operating within the SUTURA ecosystem.

## **Synthesis**

The reviewed literature and existing systems demonstrate that while digital solutions for garment management and retail exist, they fail to meet the specific operational needs of local tailoring enterprises. The Tailor Works provides a sophisticated designer-led model but lacks the high-volume walk-in tracking and affordable infrastructure required by neighborhood shops. Tom Tailor showcases a robust omnichannel framework, yet it is restricted to finished goods and cannot handle the one-of-one production logic or raw material tracking essential to custom tailoring. Alterations Plus offers a strong localized service network in the Philippines, but it functions primarily as an internal service-provider tool without a client-facing interface for measurement management or automated tracking.

None of the reviewed systems provides a unified technical workflow that integrates high-volume order tracking, inventory manangement, and measurement versioning in a single platform. These consistent gaps establish the functional necessity for SUTURA. By implementing a Subscription and Admin Module alongside a Customer Job and Fulfillment Order Module, SUTURA bridges the gap between high-end customization and daily shop efficiency. Furthermore, its specialized Inventory and Supplier Management Module addresses the specific materials tracking needs identified in the literature, while the Centralized Analytics Dashboard provides the business intelligence that general retail systems lack. Ultimately, SUTURA is designed to professionalize the Philippine tailoring sector by offering a comprehensive, integrated management solution specifically calibrated for the unique production workflows of local craftsmen.

# **METHODology**

The development of SUTURA adopts a Developmental Research Approach using the Agile Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC). This approach is highly suitable for the proposed system because it manages a complex architectural framework divided into multiple specialized modules, such as Supplier Management, Order Fulfillment, and Centralized Analytics. Since SUTURA is designed to bridge the functional gaps in existing tailoring systems by unifying walk-in tracking and measurement versioning, the Agile methodology provides the necessary flexibility to refine these unique features through iterative cycles.

The development process is broken down into specific sprint cycles, with each cycle producing a working version of the system’s features. Rather than building everything at once, each module is designed, tested, and polished before moving to the next. This iterative approach allows for constant feedback and improvements, making it easier to adjust to new requirements. The project starts by gathering data through interviews and observations with tailoring shop owners, employees, and customers. The researchers examine current manual tasks like tracking orders, managing fabric stock, and filing customer measurements to identify common problems and inefficiencies. This information establishes the foundation for the system's technical and functional requirements, ensuring the software directly addresses the specific needs of the tailoring business.

The project moves into planning and design by organizing the gathered features into a prioritized backlog based on their importance and technical needs. The system is then modeled using use case diagrams, BPMN workflows, activity diagrams, sequence diagrams, database design, and user interface prototypes. These technical blueprints serve as a detailed guide for development, ensuring every part of the system stays aligned with the project goals.

During the actual development phase, the system is built through consecutive sprints, starting with the setup of secure login credentials and role-based access for different users. Subsequent cycles focus on building the core subscription and admin tools alongside the garment order and fulfillment modules to handle digital tracking and measurement history. Later sprints introduce the appointment scheduling system, inventory controls for fabric and supplies, and the billing system for financial transparency. The centralized analytics dashboard is integrated to provide real-time reports on sales and productivity, completing the platform’s functional requirements.

To maintain high quality, the researchers perform continuous testing during every sprint. Each feature is tested individually and as part of the whole system to ensure everything works correctly. Selected tailoring shop owners and staff then participate in User Acceptance Testing (UAT) to check if the system is easy to use and helpful for their daily tasks. The feedback from these tests is used to fix issues and improve the software in the next sprint, making the final platform reliable and practical.

Once the sprints are complete, the system is launched in a cloud environment for easy access. After deployment, the researchers continue to monitor the platform to fix bugs, improve speed, and ensure data security. Regular maintenance and future updates are also scheduled based on user feedback to keep the system efficient and up to date for tailoring shops.

**Requirement Gathering **

The requirements gathering for SUTURA involves a comprehensive data collection process using semi-structured interviews and direct on-site observations within the local tailoring industry. The researchers engaged with owners and staff from established tailoring shops in Davao City to analyze their current manual workflows and identify recurring operational bottlenecks. These specific businesses were chosen as representatives of the small-scale garment sector that still relies heavily on traditional paper-based tracking.

To ensure comfortable and accurate communication, the interviews were held using a mix of Bisaya, Tagalog, and English, based on what the participants preferred. The researchers documented all responses, which were then transcribed and translated into English for formal analysis. During this process, the text was carefully refined to ensure the technical requirements remained clear while strictly maintaining the original intent and context provided by the tailoring shop staff.

The assessment showed that tailoring operations rely almost entirely on physical documentation and manual coordination. Orders and customer measurements are typically recorded in handwritten logbooks, while communication for fabric sourcing and job updates happens through scattered phone calls and messages without a unified tracking system. This reliance on paper-based methods leads to significant issues, including misplaced customer records, difficulty in tracking material stock, and errors in calculating final costs.

The shop’s management acts as the main hub for all tasks, from taking measurements to handling payments. Relying on a single person or small team to manage thousands of orders especially during peak school uniform seasons it creates massive operational strain. This high workload often leads to miscommunication between the front desk and tailors, resulting in late schedules, incorrect garment sizes, and lost manual records.

The assignment of tasks and the production flow are managed through informal, experience-based methods. Customer orders are typically handled using a first-in, first-out logic, with adjustments made based on the specific skills of the tailors and the urgency of the deadline. However, this process depends entirely on the owner’s memory and staff awareness rather than a structured digital queue. Without a formal system to track progress, this leads to inconsistent job prioritization, difficulties in monitoring the status of individual garments, and frequent scheduling conflicts during high-volume periods.

Difficulties in managing timelines and tracking orders were identified as a recurring issue for tailoring shops. Overlapping deadlines and the manual checking of staff availability lead to unpredictable production flows and long turnaround times. Because these schedules are managed across multiple physical notebooks and folders, errors and conflicts frequently occur, making it difficult to handle several high-priority orders at once.

Payment recording and labor cost tracking are also handled through manual entry. All financial transactions and deposits are recorded in physical notebooks or sales logs, with tailor payments typically computed on a per-piece basis. While this manual process works for a small volume of orders, it becomes highly inefficient and prone to calculation errors as the number of transactions increases.

Customer limitations were also identified, as customers currently lack access to real-time status updates, digital measurement history, or automated notifications regarding their orders. This leads to a lack of convenience and frequent inquiries via phone or walk-ins just to check on a garment's progress.

To address these operational gaps, SUTURA was designed as a centralized platform that streamlines the entire tailoring lifecycle. The system provides digital measurement storage, automated production scheduling, and a structured management feature to track garments from cutting to final quality control. It integrates financial modules for deposit tracking, per-piece labor calculations, and material inventory management to replace error-prone manual logs. Furthermore, SUTURA includes automated customer notifications and a progress-tracking interface, allowing customers to monitor their order status in real-time and improving the overall accessibility of tailoring services.

**Requirements Analysis **

Within the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC), the requirements analysis phase is essential for pinpointing and establishing the precise needs of both the users and the software. For the proposed system, Sutura: A Web-based Subscription Tailoring Business Management System with Supplier Management, this stage concentrates on eliminating the bottlenecks associated with traditional, manual tailoring workflows by designing a unified, automated digital platform.

The system involves four main users: the system administrator, tailoring shop owners or managers, the tailoring staff, and the customers. The administrators manage the platform subscriptions and user access. Shop owners or managers oversee business analytics, inventory, and staff assignments. Tailoring staff handle measurements and production workflows, and customers can browse shops, place orders, and track garment progress in real-time.

The system operates within a web-based environment accessible through standard web browsers. Shop owners and tailoring staff primarily access the system via desktop interfaces for comprehensive management and data entry, while the platform features a mobile-responsive design for customers. This allows clients to browse garment designs, submit tailoring requests, and monitor their order progress remotely from their mobile devices. The system is specifically designed for use within the physical premises of registered tailoring shops in Davao City, as well as remotely by their respective clients.

The system must support key functional requirements, including a real-time ordering system and automated production tracking. Timing and triggers are central to the system’s operation: notifications are triggered instantly when an order is placed or when a garment moves to a new production stage, such as cutting or final fitting. The platform also facilitates comprehensive supplier tracking alongside internal task assignments. It supports flexible billing through cash and digital gateways like GCash and Maya, integrated with a SaaS-based subscription module for efficient operation management.

Non-functional requirements include reliability, security, usability, and scalability. The system shall have 99.9% uptime and perform automatic database backups every 24 hours, protect sensitive information, and ensure an intuitive experience for all users. It is deployed in a cloud-based environment to provide high availability and real-time synchronization, allowing the system to handle multiple tailoring enterprises in Davao City efficiently.

In conclusion, this analysis ensures that Sutura is strategically aligned with the users' needs and the specific working environment of the local tailoring industry, providing a strong roadmap for the subsequent design and technical implementation of the platform.

## **Technical Background**

· **Technologies to be Used**

The development of Sutura: A Web-Based Subscription Tailoring Business Management System with Supplier Management will utilize modern, accessible, and industry-relevant technologies aligned with current standards in web application development. The system will adopt a client-server architecture, enabling centralized data management and multi-user access for tailoring shop owners, staff, and suppliers through a single unified platform. The system will be fully responsive, ensuring optimal usability across desktop computers, tablets, and mobile browsers without requiring a separate mobile application.

The system will utilize Next.js, React, and TypeScript to build a dynamic and strictly-typed frontend. This stack, paired with Tailwind CSS, ensures a professional and fully responsive interface that adapts seamlessly to any screen size. To manage complex data flows and ensure a smooth user experience, Zustand will be utilized for efficient, lightweight state management across all system modules.

On the backend, the system will utilize Laravel (PHP) to manage all server-side operations, including business logic, user authentication, role-based access control, and data processing. Laravel will expose RESTful APIs to facilitate seamless communication between the frontend interface and the server, ensuring efficient and real-time data exchange across all system modules such as order tracking, inventory monitoring, and supplier management.

For data storage, MySQL will serve as the primary relational database management system, providing a structured and reliable schema for managing customer profiles, body measurements, job orders, inventory records, supplier information, billing records, and transaction history. MySQL is well-suited for Sutura's operations as it supports structured relational data, which is essential for linking customer orders to inventory consumption and supplier procurement records.

The database will be hosted on PlanetScale, a scalable cloud-based MySQL hosting platform, ensuring data availability, security, and remote accessibility without the need for on-premise server infrastructure.

For deployment, the frontend application will be hosted using Vercel, a cloud hosting platform optimized for modern web applications, while the Laravel backend and its API services will be deployed on Railway or Render. These platforms ensure that the system remains accessible online throughout the development and testing phases of the project.

For the development environment, the team will use Visual Studio Code as the primary code editor due to its extensive support for PHP, JavaScript, and the frameworks used in this project.

GitHub will be utilized for version control and collaborative development, allowing team members to manage, track, and merge code changes efficiently throughout the development lifecycle.

Postman will be used to test and validate all RESTful API endpoints, ensuring system reliability, data integrity, and consistent performance before integration.

Additionally, Figma will be used for designing user interface wireframes and interactive prototypes prior to development, serving as the visual and structural blueprint for all system screens, user flows, and design components.
